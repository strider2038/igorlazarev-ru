I want to give blogging another try. There are plenty of topics I would like to write about, but I no longer want to spend several hours writing and proofreading every single post.

<!--more-->

So I decided to try a different format: use neural networks as a practical tool. Not to write instead of me, but to help with the draft, structure, and phrasing. At this point it is enough to dictate an idea into my phone, get a rough draft back, and start from there.

I spent a long time thinking about *what exactly* I want to do myself and what I can hand over to the tools. In the end I came to a simple conclusion: I want to spend no more than half an hour on a single post. I would rather give the rest of my time to rest, family, games, experiments with vibe coding, and other things I genuinely enjoy doing. If neural networks help me fit writing into that format, then publishing **at least something** is better than going silent again for a long time.

Most likely, what appears here next will not be only full-sized articles, but also **concept notes**: an idea, some context, a few key points. If a topic catches both my own interest and the readers', it can always grow into a full article later.

There is no shortage of topics already. I want to write separately about working with files through S3, publish my presentations from the Golang course, and gradually turn each lecture into an article. And I also want to share notes about vibe coding, which I am actively learning right now.

Let's see whether this third attempt turns into a steady rhythm.
